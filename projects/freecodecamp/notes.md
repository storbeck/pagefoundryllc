# FreeCodeCamp — Developer & Curriculum Contributor Summary

This document summarizes my contributions to FreeCodeCamp during my time as a developer and curriculum contributor in 2015. My work helped shape the early front-end development certificate, guided thousands of new developers through their first real projects, and contributed to FreeCodeCamp’s growth during its foundational years.

---

## Overview

From June to October 2015, I worked directly with FreeCodeCamp’s early team and community to design core portions of the front-end learning experience. I created many of the example projects ("Ziplines" and "Basejumps") that students used as reference implementations, assisted in shaping the early curriculum structure, and mentored students through the then-active Gitter learning chat.

My work was publicly recognized by FreeCodeCamp and featured in several of their published announcements.

---

## 1. Curriculum Design & Example Projects

FreeCodeCamp’s front-end certificate originally revolved around real, hands-on projects known as **Ziplines** (simple apps) and **Basejumps** (larger full-stack or advanced front-end projects). I contributed extensively to both.

### **Key Contributions**

* **Designed and coded many of the official example projects** used as the model solutions for students.
* Helped establish the structure and difficulty progression of the early front-end curriculum.
* Worked directly with Quincy Larson and the core team to refine project requirements and UX expectations.
* Ensured the examples were not only functional but visually polished, giving students aspirational reference points.

### **Public Recognition**

FreeCodeCamp highlighted my contributions publicly:

> *“Geoff Storbeck is a camper and web developer based out of Columbus, Ohio. He designed and coded many of our Ziplines and Basejumps.”*
> — FreeCodeCamp Announcement ("Our 1,600-hour JavaScript Coding Curriculum")

---

## 2. Student Mentorship & Community Support

A major part of FreeCodeCamp’s early success came from its active, supportive community. I contributed by:

* Providing daily help to students via the **Gitter learning chat**, the primary support channel at the time.
* Reviewing code, giving feedback, debugging issues, and helping students learn through hands-on guidance.
* Answering questions and clarifying project requirements, especially for front-end challenges.

This work helped hundreds of new developers gain confidence, improve their skills, and complete key certification projects.
