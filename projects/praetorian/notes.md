# Frontend Engineering Contributions at Praetorian (Chariot Platform)

This document summarizes the scope, depth, and impact of the frontend engineering work completed during my tenure at Praetorian, specifically on the Chariot offensive security platform. It captures the core responsibilities, major initiatives, architectural contributions, UI/UX improvements, and cross‑team collaboration efforts that shaped the platform’s evolution.

## Overview

As a Staff Frontend Engineer on the Chariot platform, I was responsible for leading and delivering key UI/UX initiatives across the application. My work focused on improving developer velocity, strengthening architectural consistency, elevating user experience, and helping define modern standards across the frontend codebase.

My contributions spanned:

* Large‑scale UI/UX redesigns
* Component architecture modernization

* Developer-onboarding improvements
* Performance tuning
* Reliability and regression workflows

## Major Areas of Contribution

### **1. Full Frontend Architecture & Platform Rewrite**

As the Staff Frontend Engineer for Chariot, I led the complete architectural redesign and rebuild of the platform’s frontend. This was a high‑intensity, multi‑year effort that transformed Chariot from a legacy system with deep technical debt into a modern, lean, and maintainable application.

Key contributions:

* **Architected the frontend from scratch**, establishing the core patterns, conventions, and standards used across the entire codebase.
* **Delivered a high‑fidelity implementation of new Figma designs**, ensuring pixel‑accurate, consistent UI throughout the application.
* **Designed an ultra‑lean architecture**, reducing onboarding time for new engineers from *months* (on the legacy platform) to *a few days*.
* **Built and maintained a Storybook‑driven component library**, tailored to match design specs perfectly while keeping product code extremely minimal.
* **Enabled AI‑assisted development** by structuring components, utilities, and patterns so they were readable and predictable for LLM‑based coding agents.
* **Served as the engineering lead during the rewrite**, coordinating patterns, providing guidance, and ensuring continuity across teams.

### **2. UI/UX Redesigns and Experience Improvements**

I led the holistic redesign of Chariot’s user experience, ensuring that the frontend rewrite delivered not only cleaner code but also a significantly more intuitive interface.

Key improvements:

* **Modernized every major workflow** with new layouts, typography, and interaction patterns.
* **Created consistent design systems** for spacing, grids, drawers, modals, tables, and navigation.
* **Improved clarity and cognitive load** in complex security workflows by refining hierarchy and information density.
* **Implemented highly interactive views** optimized for offensive security teams working with large datasets.

These improvements formed the new foundation of Chariot’s UX and enabled future features to be added cleanly and predictably.
