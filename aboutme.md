My Journey Through the Industry
Praetorian — Staff Frontend Engineer (Chariot Platform)

I led the full frontend rewrite of Praetorian’s offensive cybersecurity platform, going from a slow, legacy interface to a modern, component-driven Vue/React architecture.

Reduced onboarding from months to days.

Delivered a new design system and studio-quality UI.

Helped the platform move from monthly releases to same-day deployments.

Worked directly with capability teams, security engineers, and leadership to modernize the entire developer experience.

JPMorgan Chase — Lead UI/UX Developer & Application Developer

Across nearly a decade, I worked my way up from application developer to UI lead, shipping tools used across the entire firm.

Some highlights:

Cassandra as a Service: Designed and built the self-service UI for internal Cassandra clusters; rewrote a legacy Spring Boot + Angular 1.x app into a modern React/Redux interface.

Account Lifecycle Management: Was the very first developer on the project, shipped the MVP to production, then led the UI architecture for a suite of five internal apps.

GROOT: Built graph visualization tools for mapping technology systems across the firm.

Move Money: Built the real-time dashboard for tracking high-value transaction flows.

JPMorgan taught me how to build enterprise-grade systems that have to be correct, resilient, and secure — without sacrificing usability.

FreeCodeCamp — Developer & Curriculum Contributor

I helped design FreeCodeCamp’s early front-end curriculum and created many of the original example projects (“Ziplines” and “Basejumps”), some of which were featured on CodePen’s front page with 40k–50k views each.

I mentored students in the Gitter chat, helped refine project requirements, and contributed to the early growth of what became the world’s largest free coding curriculum.

Before All That — Systems Administration & Infrastructure

I started my career long before the term “full-stack developer” became popular.

At Jumpline, I:

This gave me a deep understanding of the systems that everything else runs on — something that continues to shape how I build frontends that are robust, observable, and grounded in reality.
